Bhagat Singh

Highlights Bhagat Singh's life:

  • Born - 1907, Banga, Punjab, British India (present-day Punjab, Pakistan)
  • Jallianwala Bagh massacre - At a young age of just 12, Bhagat Sing visited the Jallianwala Bagh hours after the tragic massacre.
  • Education - Bhagat Sing joined the National College in Lahore in 1923.
  • Naujawan Bharat Sabha - Formed by Bhagat Singh in 1926.
  • CLA bombing - Bhagat Singh led the non-violent bombings in the Central Legislative Assembly in 1929.
  • Hunger strike - After being re-arrested and moved to Central Jail Mianwali from the Delhi jail, Bhagat Singh led a hunger strike against the discrimination amongst Indians and European prisoners and for being recognised as political prisoners.
  • Execution- On 23rd March 1931, Bhagat Singh was hanged to death after moving the schedule ahead by 11 hours.
